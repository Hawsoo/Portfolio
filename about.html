A Message out of Love From Our Team

So are you having a good time here?

Being a bunch of amateur programmers, we sure fit the bill of the word 'amateur.' Don't you think so?

By the way, do you know what 'amateur' means?

Yeah sure, it means 'not profressional', but the word in of itself is intriguing and makes you think. First off, the word itself is derived from Latin, of course, and it's easy to tell that because of its similarity to the word 'amour' (to love) in French. So in essence, 'amateur' is derived from 'to love'.

That's great and all, but if you ever took French, you'll realize that words like 'acteur' (actor), and 'ingénieur' (engineer) have the 'eur' suffix to it, indicating a profession. 'Amateur' has that same suffix. So think of this: what do you call a professional that does their craft out of love?

An amateur.
